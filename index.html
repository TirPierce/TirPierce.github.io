Aftercare

  • How to care for your tattoo?

    Change the wrap every 3 hours. When changing the wrap, it is very important to wash your hands first and then wash your new tattoo with luke warm water. Dab dry with kitchen towel. After dabbing dry apply a thin layer of Pegasus/Bepanthen cream evenly across your tattoo and re-wrap your tattoo with cling film (available in food stores) Repeat this process for three to four days. Stay out of the sunlight and tanning booths until the tattoo is healed, at least 10-14 days. Do not soak your tattoo in the bath, sauna, jacuzzi or go swimming while your new tattoo is healing. Showers are fine. Do not rub or pick the treated area while it is healing. Loss of colour and/or infection could occur. Your tattoo should heal about two weeks. Over the course of these two weeks keep it clean and continue to apply the Pegasus/Bepanthen cream evenly on your tattoo. Any issues with your new tattoo or concerns, please don’t hesitate go to get in contact with the studio.

  • How to care for your piercing?

    How your piercing heals is up to you. Chemicals will not make you heal faster! Quite in the opposite in fact, chemicals of any kind will upset the balance within your newly forming cells and create a condition in which your piercing will heal tremendously slow. Use Saline solution, it’s the key to quick healing! It’s PH balanced to our bodies. The easiest and most accurate way to acquire saline is in the form of saline solution for contact lenses Non-iodized sea salt will always state on the package ‘’this salt does not contain iodine, a necessary nutrient’’. Check the label before you purchase. DO NOT USE table salt or Epson salt. Always wash your hands before touching your piercing Mix 1/4 teaspoon of sea salt with half pint of boiling water, wait for the solution to cool then using a cotton bud, gently clean around your piercing to remove any lymph (crusts) away from the piercing, do this 3 to 4 times a day, then gently dry the piercing. Do not touch or twist the jewellery For oral piercings (inside your mouth) Use an alcohol free mouth wash every time you eat, drink or smoke. It’s recommended to keep using the mouthwash until you return for your change down to the correct size jewellery

  • Laser tattoo removal aftercare.

    Immediately after the treatment, you may notice the treatment area is red and swollen and will feel much like a sunburn. During the healing process, the treatment site may also form a crust, scab or even blisters. This is all part if the healing process. Failing to allow normal healing of the treatment site will increase the possibility of scar formation and can lead to infection. It may be necessary to use a dressing to protect the area if there is a risk of damage. We recommend using some alovera gel on the site 3-4times a day for the following 5-7days and if blisters form and burst change to an antiseptic cream to prevent the site becoming infected. Most activities can be resumed immediately after treatment however, soaking (I.E Baths and swimming) the removal site should be avoided until healing iix complete. It is very important that the area be protected from sun damage, As the site heals, newly formed skin will be more susceptible to sun damage, Under normal conditions, the process which breaks down the tattoo ink begins 2 weeks after the laser treatment. The ink will continue to evacuate until about the 6week post treatment mark. For this reason, treatments are scheduled no closer than 4-6 weeks.

Tattoo FAQ

  • Do I need to make an appointment?

    We do recommend to book in as we are a busy studio and can’t always guarantee we will have space available for walk-ins, however we always welcome them when we can.

    .

  • How do I make an appointment?

    You can visit the studio anytime from 12-7 pm from Monday to Sunday and we can have a consultation and arrange a booking for you. Alternatively we can have a chat over the email or our Facebook page and we can guide you through the process. We do require a deposit to book you in and the deposit will vary depending on the size of your piece. This deposit will come off your final price of the tattoo. When booking in please provide us with reference images of what you are looking to get done along with the size and placement. If you don’t have any images, give us a clear description and include as much information as possible so we can assure we book you in with the right artists for the right amount of time.

    .

  • Is a consultation necessary?

    It is not necessary but we do advise to book it in when you are booked in for bigger pieces and projects so you can discuss your ideas with the artist.

    .

  • What is your hourly rate? / What is your shop minimum?

    As we are a custom shop we charge by the design. Our shop minimum is €80 and our day rates range between €700-€900. Please note that any quotes given to you over the email or Facebook are rough estimates and a final price will be given to you at a consultation only. What do you mean when you say Dublin Ink is a custom studio? Custom means we do not provide you with a range of readymade tattoos to choose from, Dublin Ink try to provide each client with a personalised piece instead.

    .

  • Is there age restrictions for tattoos?

    We have a strict 18+ policy in the shop for tattoos and a valid proof of ID is required on the day of your tattoo.

    .

  • How do I prepare for my tattoo appointment?

    It is very important that you have a good night sleep before coming in to have a tattoo done and that you don’t go out drinking the night before. We recommend to take a shower before coming in and wearing clean and comfortable clothing as careful hygiene practice starts the minute you walk in the door. When choosing your outfit be mindful of the placement of your tattoo, you might have to take off certain items of clothing to make things more comfortable for the tattoo and yourself as Dublin Ink is an open studio. Make sure you have food before the appointment and that you bring snacks and a bottle of water with you as well.

    .

  • How do I look after my new tattoo?

    We provide every client with a special wrap called Saniderm. You can read more about it here.

    .

  • I have a medical condition, can I still be tattooed?

    In almost all cases, yes, however there are a few exceptions to this rule. So for your safety, if you are in doubt= ask your G.P for a doctor’s note. This will outline your health status in relation to a tattoo and help us give you the correct after-care. If you have a rash, burn, broken skin, or sunburn in the area that will be tattooed, you must wait until the skin has healed before we can tattoo you. It is possible to tattoo over scar tissue but an in person consultation is required to determine if that is possible.

    .

  • Can I get tattooed if I’m pregnant or breastfeeding?

    Due to health reason we will not tattoo you if you are pregnant or breastfeeding, if you would like to know more about this let our staff know and they will be happy to assist you.

    .

  • Can I bring my child/ren to the store with me?

    We are child friendly however if you are the one getting the tattoo it is required that there is another adult that can mind your children, as our duty is to care for you and not your children. We ask that you consider the circumstances when bringing any minors with you.

    .

  • Where is your shop located?

    We are here! Located in old city Temple Bar on Cows Lane just beside the new Hard Rock Hotel.

    .

  • What is your cancellation policy?

    We do have a 72h cancellation policy in order for your deposit to be refunded or booking rescheduled. Failing to give us the 72h notice before your appointment will result in your deposit to be lost.

    .

Piercing FAQ

  • Do I need to make an appointment?

    Our piercing appointments can be both walk-ins and booked appointments.

    .

  • How do I make an appointment?

    You can massage the shop or our piercer directly.

    .

  • How old do you have to be?

    16+ with photo I.D. for most piercings (18+nipple and oral piercings) 14+ with a parent or legal guardian present for lobe and basic cartilage piercings.

    We offer a wide variety of piercing services to minors. If you are 16+ you can get most of the piercings if you bring a valid I.D with you such as a passport, state I.D. card or age card. If the minor is 14+ they will need to be accompanied by a parent or a legal guardian and both of you will need to provide I.D. If you are the parent of a minor, but you and the minor have different last names, we need documentation showing why your names are different (ie. divorce, name change, etc.) or some other legal documentation demonstrating guardianship. The policies have been established in order to protect minors, their parents and our studio.

    .

  • Can I come in for a consultation?

    Absolutely! Swing by and meet our piercer, see the studio and ask questions and discuss your ideas with our piercer. There may be a short wait but our piercer is always happy to take time and have a chat with you.

    .

  • What jewellery do you use for piercings?

    We only use implant grade titanium for piercings which is compliant with the ASTM F136 t or ISO 5832-3 standard.

    .

  • Can I bring my own jewellery to be pierced with?

    We don’t pierce with materials we cannot verify the origins of, there is a risk of developing an allergy or infection if the jewellery is not safe for initial piercing.

    .

  • When can I change my jewellery?

    Everyone heals at a different rate, which is why we do check-up appointments. Your piercer can evaluate the progress of healing and let you know if the piercing is ready to be changed and help you change it as well.

    .

  • I have a medical condition, can I still be pierced?

    In almost all cases, yes, however there are a few exceptions to this rule. So for your safety, if you are in doubt: ask your G.P for a doctor’s note. This will outline your health status in relation to a piercing and help us give you the correct after-care. If you have a rash, burn, broken skin, or sunburn in the area that will be pierced, you must wait until the skin has healed before we can pierce you. It is possible to pierce through scar tissue but an in person consultation is required to determine if that is possible.

    .

  • Can I get a piercing if I’m pregnant or breastfeeding?

    Due to health reasons we will not pierce you if you are pregnant or breastfeeding, if you would like to know more about this let our staff know and they will be happy to assist you.

    .

  • I have breast implants, can I still get my nipples pierced?

    This decision is yours to make after receiving advice from your surgeon. Generally once you have received all your follow up appointments there will be no issues. However some surgeons recommend waiting longer.

    .

  • I have lip fillers, can I still get a lip piercing?

    The answer is no. If you had lip fillers we cannot do any piercings in or around it due to health reasons. If we pierce an area where the fillers were injected there is a risk of infection, migration and loss of your piercing due to the fillers restricting the blood flow in the area.

    .

  • Can I bring my child/ren to the store with me?

    We are child friendly however if you are the one getting pierced it is required that there is another adult that can mind your children, as our duty is to care for you and not your children. We ask that you consider the circumstances when bringing any minors with you.

    .
